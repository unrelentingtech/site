text = "Moving away from Munin For years I’ve been using Munin on my systems for graphs on system resource usage. I’ve just recently discovered PyMunin, a python based extension that makes munin even more awesome. But as always, I’ve encountered with limitations and problems. First of all, Munin plugins are ugly. They come in various languages, bash, Perl, Python, and so on, they does not share any similarities and sometimes they even have dependencies not mentioned during the install or in the readme. Munin only has 5 minutes resolution – this is OK for some cases, but today’s requirements are changing, and it’s really hard to change. Also, the munin-node is basically periodical: at every 5 minute it runs through the changes and compute the required data which result a small CPU usage spike. So I decided to go for something lighter, that runs as a daemon and does not require hacked plugins to work. The result is: collectd. Why collectd? In short, it’s probaly the most light-weight, modest and really modular data collector. It does not generate any graphs – that’s done by something else, on-demand. It’s also very portable, suitable for both large scale computing ( could be source for nagios ) and for embedded as well. And it’s fairly easy to configure.
